
PECULIAR WORDS AND
PHRASES
 AN angry Cornishman would formerly say when in anger, "I shall push a stone in his cairn," meaning he shall see him buried.
"Curri mi clack er du cuirn" is the expression of a Scotch Highlander (Labbach's Prehistoric Times).
This is interpreted as a mark of respect, or, I will do something to build you a monument.
In the parish of Breage, the habit of prefixing names to the people is common.
The "Tubby" Prichard's are well known.
"Alsie's' children are common.
"Scaw" was applied to several families.
